DUNYA-E-ISLAM (WORLD OF ISLAM)

Fears of Renewed War Between Arabs and Jews

LONDON, 27 December: British Foreign Secretary Mr. Armont Baboon discussed the Southern Palestine crisis with experts yesterday. Following the resurgence of fighting in the region known as Nagheeb, Mr. Baboon analyzed reports from Cairo. While the Arab public remains reluctant toward open warfare, their final stance will be determined by their representatives.

Egyptian Prime Minister Nuqrashi Pasha Assassinated

28 December: Prime Minister Nuqrashi Pasha was fatally shot six times while entering the Foreign Affairs office in Cairo. A youth named Abdul Majeed, a student at Fawad University, was arrested at the scene. It is heavily suspected that the attack is linked to the recently dissolved Muslim Brotherhood organization. Security forces have cordoned off the entire district as investigations intensify to identify co-conspirators. The 60-year-old leader of the Sa'adi Party had previously survived multiple threats, but this assassination has left the government in a state of high alert.

King Abdullah: Protector of Christians, Official Statement

During the Christmas Eve celebrations in Nazareth, King Abdullah's representatives issued a statement assuring Christian communities of their protection. Even as sirens were triggered by unidentified aircraft overhead, the mass proceeded without interruption. Brigadier General William Railway attended the ceremony where Colonel Abdullah personally greeted the religious leaders, reaffirming that the Kingdom remains the primary guardian of all sacred sites across the territory.

New Grand Mufti for Palestine Appointed Amidst Resignations

OMAN: King Abdullah has appointed Sheikh Hussain Barallah as the new Mufti of Palestine, replacing Amin Hussaini. Sheikh Barallah takes office during a wave of administrative shifts. In a related development, Social Affairs Minister Abdul Hadi and Education Minister Ikram Dastoor have submitted their resignations. These exits reflect internal tensions regarding the future administrative direction of the region amidst these new appointments.

ADABIYAT (LITERATURE)

Farmoodāt-e-Hazrat ‘Ārif

1. They will come if meeting is in destiny; why search wretched houses?

2. Mercy from a killer is useless if the victim has already lost their breath.

3. If you won't come, say so; the pain of waiting has lasted since that day.

4. Why step from the veil? Let cruelty continue while you remain hidden.

5. If I ask for mercy, ward off this calamity; confess now, deny it later.

6. The cup never stays empty, O ascetic; God knows how it fills again.

7. Why pray for death when calamities already crowd this full house?

8. Let my longings stay in my destiny rather than becoming my enemy's fortune.

9. I could extinguish Hell in a moment with the river of sins I carry.

10. God knows where desires will be met; there is no hope in this world or the next.
